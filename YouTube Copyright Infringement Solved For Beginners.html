YouTube Copyright Infringement Solved For Beginners

Hello guys, today I'm going to give a few pointers for the new youtubers out there who has some good content but don't know what to look out for. For instance anybody who started their youtube experience has one or the other time experience the "Copyright infringement" warning from youtube, and that is what we are going to be talking about today.


Copyright Infringement warning from youtube means that you have used materials in your video content that doesn't belong to you like for example the most common material used is music tracks or audio tracks, which you might have incorporated into your videos because it makes the video more interesting and complete. Mainstream music and other popular music cannot be used in videos on youtube if it isn't your own creation and to used it, you must first required copyright privileges to those musics or tracks from the original owners and creators.


If you unknowingly used such kind of musics or audio tracks without copyright privileges and then you posted it on youtube, then youtube will issue a copyright strike against your videos with some punishments ranging from inability to monetized the said video to getting it taken down by Youtube. A couple more of these strikes will lead to suspension of your Youtube channel.


If you have been sent a "copyright Infringement" warning on any of your videos then there are a few options which you can take to resolve it.

1) You can delete the video yourself from your youtube channel.

2) You can remove the materials that are causing the copyright Infringement in this case removing the audio or music tracks and your video is left without any sound.

3) You can replace the audio or music track from the video with a replacement audio from youtube music library. (but if you have listen to any of these audio track you will know that they are very bland and boring)

Note: You cannot replace the audio track of the video with another one from your own personal library. You need to that in your video maker software or app first and then re-upload the new video as brand new, losing all the views and likes in the process)


So if you still want to put music on your videos and not have to worry about the pesky copyright problems then you can do a few things to avoid it.

You can either create your own music and tracks (if you have the time and talent), or you do a cover of the songs you want to use, or you can use a no copyright songs. 


Here, the third option of using the no copyright songs is what I want to talk about. No copyright songs are like any other music tracks with vocals and music track but like the name suggests there is no copyright strike if you used it on your videos. You just have to choose the music you like and download it and use it just like that and you can save a lot more time.
